I've been collecting tweets that mention @HillaryClinton and @realDonaldTrump for the last several days: before, during, and after the first debate. I thought it would be interesting to do a simple look at what Twitter has to say the candidates are. In particular, I looked at statements that looked like "[CANDIDATE] is [SOMETHING]." I was interested, for example, in statements such as "@HillaryClinton is a crook." But not statements such as, "I hate that @HillaryClinton is a crook." In other words, I tried to find very explicit declarations people made about the candidates. Follow along in the code below and read the results at the bottom.

The regex's below are the meat of how I select the phrases. I'm specifically looking for sentences that start with the candidate's handle. I capture all words, spaces, and commas after the word "is" up to a period. This should allow for things like, "@realDonaldTrump is a liar, a phony, and a cheat." and capture "a liar, a phony, and a cheat." I'm likely missing a lot because this assumes people on Twitter are using periods to end sentences. The regex also doesn't match anything that contains more than word characters, spaces, or commas. It could also lead to some confusing results if people use a period before the end of the sentence. But, regex's are hard, this is a first pass, let's do this.

In [8]:
h_regex = r"((^|(?<=[.:?!]\s))@HillaryClinton is ([\w\s]+)\.)"
d_regex = r"((^|(?<=[.:?!]\s))@realDonaldTrump is ([\w\s]+)\.)"
hillary_matched = hillary_tweets.text.str.extract(h_regex, expand=True).dropna()
donald_matched = donald_tweets.text.str.extract(d_regex, expand=True).dropna()
