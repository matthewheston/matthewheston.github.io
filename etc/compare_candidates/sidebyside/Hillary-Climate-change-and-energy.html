"color: #0057b8; font-family: Unity; font-size: 16px; font-weight: 700; line-height: 1.1; letter-spacing: 0em; margin-top: 20px; margin-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-left: 0px; display: inline;">Climate change and clean energy


"color: #33333; font-size: 2.0em; font-family: Unity; font-style: normal; font-weight: bold; display: inline; margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 30px; padding-top: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-left: 0px; line-height: 1.1;"> Making America the world’s clean energy superpower and meeting the climate challenge.

"color: #333333; font-size: 1.4em; font-family: Unity; font-style: normal; font-weight: bold; line-height: 1.2; margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 30px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-left: 0px;">Hillary will:

  • "color: #333333; font-size: 1.1em; font-family: Georgia; font-style: normal; font-weight: normal; line-height: 1.2; margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-left: 0px; border-top: 0px solid #cccccc; border-bottom: 0px solid #cccccc;"> Create good-paying jobs by making the United States the clean energy superpower of the 21st century.

  • "color: #333333; font-size: 1.1em; font-family: Georgia; font-style: normal; font-weight: normal; line-height: 1.2; margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-left: 0px; border-top: 0px solid #cccccc; border-bottom: 0px solid #cccccc;"> Set national goals to have 500 million solar panels installed; generate enough renewable energy to power every home in America; cut energy waste in homes, schools, and hospitals by a third; and reduce American oil consumption by a third.

  • "color: #333333; font-size: 1.1em; font-family: Georgia; font-style: normal; font-weight: normal; line-height: 1.2; margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-left: 0px; border-top: 0px solid #cccccc; border-bottom: 0px solid #cccccc;">Lead the world in the fight against climate change by bringing greenhouse gas emissions to 30 percent below what they were in 2005 within the next decade—and keep going.

"color: #666666; font-family: Georgia; font-style: italic; font-size: 20px; font-weight: 400; margin: 0px; line-height: 1.2;">"I won’t let anyone take us backward, deny our economy the benefits of harnessing a clean energy future, or force our children to endure the catastrophe that would result from unchecked climate change."

"color: #b2b2b2; font-family: Unity; font-size: 10px; line-height: 1.1; letter-spacing: 0.2em; margin: 0px; display: inline; text-transform: uppercase;">Hillary, NOVEMBER 29, 2015

"color: #333333; font-size: 1.1em; font-family: Georgia; font-style: normal; font-weight: normal; line-height: 1.2; margin-top: 10px; margin-bottom: 5px; padding-top: 10px; padding-bottom: 10px; padding-right: 0px; padding-left: 0px; border-top: 0px solid #cccccc; border-bottom: 0px solid #cccccc;">Climate change is an urgent threat and a defining challenge of our time—and Hillary Clinton has a plan to tackle it by making America the world’s clean energy superpower, taking bold steps to slash carbon pollution at home and around the world, and ensuring no Americans are left out or left behind as we rapidly build a clean energy economy.

2015 was the warmest year on record—and 15 of the 16 hottest years on record have come just since 2001. Already, American families are seeing the impacts of climate change with their own eyes, from the record drought in California to the frequently flooded streets of Miami and Annapolis. While climate deniers continue to ignore settled science because it does not suit their political agenda, and climate defeatists doubt America’s ability to meet this challenge, Hillary knows that America is fully up to the task.

Already, U.S. carbon pollution has been cut to its lowest level since 1995. Wind power has expanded three fold and solar power 30-fold since 2008. But that’s not enough to tackle the climate challenge, meet America’s carbon pollution reduction goals, or to compete for the $13.5 trillion of global clean energy investment unlocked by the historic international climate change agreement reached in Paris. We can and must go further.

"color: #333333; font-size: 1.4em; font-family: Unity; font-style: normal; font-weight: bold; line-height: 1.2; margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 30px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-left: 0px;">That’s why on day one, Hillary will set bold, national goals that will be achieved within ten years of her taking office:

  • "color: #333; font-size: 1.4em; font-family: Unity; font-style: normal; font-weight: bold; line-height: 1.1; margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-left: 0px; border-top: 0px solid #cccccc; border-bottom: 0px solid #cccccc;">"https://www.hillaryclinton.com/documents/11/Hillary_Clinton_Climate_Change_Fact_Sheet.pdf">Generate enough renewable energy to power every home in America, with half a billion solar panels installed by the end of Hillary’s first term.

  • "color: #333; font-size: 1.4em; font-family: Unity; font-style: normal; font-weight: bold; line-height: 1.1; margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-left: 0px; border-top: 0px solid #cccccc; border-bottom: 0px solid #cccccc;"> "https://www.hillaryclinton.com/briefing/factsheets/2016/02/02/advanced-buildings/">Cut energy waste in American homes, schools, hospitals and offices by a third and make American manufacturing the cleanest and most efficient in the world.

  • "color: #333; font-size: 1.4em; font-family: Unity; font-style: normal; font-weight: bold; line-height: 1.1; margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-left: 0px; border-top: 0px solid #cccccc; border-bottom: 0px solid #cccccc;">Reduce American oil consumption by a third through cleaner fuels and more efficient cars, boilers, ships and trucks.

"color: #333333; font-size: 1.1em; font-family: Georgia; font-style: normal; font-weight: normal; line-height: 1.2; margin-top: 0px; padding-top: 0px; padding-bottom: 20px; padding-right: 0px; padding-left: 0px;">
Hillary’s plan is designed to deliver on the pledge President Obama made at the Paris climate conference last December—without relying on climate deniers in Congress to pass new legislation. Her plan will reduce greenhouse gas emissions by up to 30 percent in 2025 relative to 2005 levels and put the country on a path to cut emissions more than 80 percent by 2050. Her approach will catalyze new investment and economic opportunity across the country, create hundreds of thousands of new jobs, reduce energy bills and save families money, make our country more secure, and protect our families and communities from pollution.

"color: #333333; font-size: 1.4em; font-family: Unity; font-style: normal; font-weight: bold; line-height: 1.2; margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 30px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-left: 0px;">As president, Hillary will:

  • "color: #333333; font-size: 1.1em; font-family: Georgia; font-style: normal; font-weight: normal; line-height: 1.2; margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-left: 0px; border-top: 0px solid #cccccc; border-bottom: 0px solid #cccccc;"> Defend, implement, and extend smart pollution and efficiency standards, including the Clean Power Plan, which will prevent 3,600 premature deaths and 90,000 asthma attacks annually, and efficiency standards for cars, trucks and appliances that are already reducing energy costs for American households and businesses by over $75 billion per year.

  • "color: #333333; font-size: 1.1em; font-family: Georgia; font-style: normal; font-weight: normal; line-height: 1.2; margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-left: 0px; border-top: 0px solid #cccccc; border-bottom: 0px solid #cccccc;"> Launch a $60 billion "https://www.hillaryclinton.com/briefing/factsheets/2015/07/26/renewable-power-vision/">Clean Energy Challenge to partner with states, cities, and rural communities and give them the tools and resources they need to go beyond federal standards in cutting carbon pollution and expanding clean energy. The Clean Energy Challenge will also help ensure all Americans share in the benefits of a clean energy economy by encouraging solar and energy efficiency investments in low-income communities.

  • "color: #333333; font-size: 1.1em; font-family: Georgia; font-style: normal; font-weight: normal; line-height: 1.2; margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-left: 0px; border-top: 0px solid #cccccc; border-bottom: 0px solid #cccccc;">Invest in clean energy infrastructure, innovation, manufacturing and workforce development to make the U.S. economy more competitive and create good paying jobs and careers. Hillary has "https://www.hillaryclinton.com/briefing/factsheets/2015/09/23/hillary-clinton-vision-for-modernizing-energy-infrastructure/">a comprehensive plan for making existing energy infrastructure cleaner and safer, unlocking new investment, and forging a climate compact with Canada and Mexico to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and accelerate clean energy deployment across the continent.

  • "color: #333333; font-size: 1.1em; font-family: Georgia; font-style: normal; font-weight: normal; line-height: 1.2; margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-left: 0px; border-top: 0px solid #cccccc; border-bottom: 0px solid #cccccc;">Ensure safe and responsible energy production. As we transition to a clean energy economy, we must ensure that the fossil fuel production taking place today is safe and responsible and that areas too sensitive for energy production are taken off the table. Hillary knows there are some places where we should keep fossil fuels in the ground or under the ocean.

  • "color: #333333; font-size: 1.1em; font-family: Georgia; font-style: normal; font-weight: normal; line-height: 1.2; margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-left: 0px; border-top: 0px solid #cccccc; border-bottom: 0px solid #cccccc;">Reform leasing on public lands. As president, Hillary would reform fossil fuel leasing and significantly expand clean energy production on public lands, from wind in Wyoming to solar in Nevada.

  • "color: #333333; font-size: 1.1em; font-family: Georgia; font-style: normal; font-weight: normal; line-height: 1.2; margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-left: 0px; border-top: 0px solid #cccccc; border-bottom: 0px solid #cccccc;">End wasteful tax subsidies for oil and gas companies. Oil and gas companies have enjoyed billions in tax breaks for decades. Hillary would end those wasteful subsidies and invest in clean energy.

  • "color: #333333; font-size: 1.1em; font-family: Georgia; font-style: normal; font-weight: normal; line-height: 1.2; margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-left: 0px; border-top: 0px solid #cccccc; border-bottom: 0px solid #cccccc;">Cut methane emissions across the economy. Hillary would cut emissions of methane, a powerful greenhouse gas, by 40-45 percent and put in place strong standards for reducing leaks from both new and existing sources.

  • "color: #333333; font-size: 1.1em; font-family: Georgia; font-style: normal; font-weight: normal; line-height: 1.2; margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; padding-right: 0px; padding-left: 0px; border-top: 0px solid #cccccc; border-bottom: 0px solid #cccccc;">Revitalize coal communities. Building a 21st century clean energy economy will create new jobs and industries, protect public health, and reduce carbon pollution. But we can’t ignore the impact this transition is already having on coal communities. Hillary’s "https://www.hillaryclinton.com/briefing/factsheets/2015/11/12/clinton-plan-to-revitalize-coal-communities/">$30 billion plan to revitalize coal communities will ensure coal miners, power plant operators, transportation workers, and their families get the respect they deserve and the benefits they have earned; invest in economic diversification and job creation; and make coal communities an engine of US economic growth in the 21st century, as they have been for generations.

"color: #666666; font-family: Georgia; font-style: italic; font-size: 20px; font-weight: 400; margin: 0px; line-height: 1.2;">"In spite of the strongest possible scientific consensus about what climate change means for our environment, economy, health, and future, there are still some who deny the facts. They’re intent on obstructing progress. We can’t let them win this fight. We have no choice. There is no Planet B."

"color: #b2b2b2; font-family: Unity; font-size: 10px; line-height: 1.1; letter-spacing: 0.2em; margin: 0px; display: inline; text-transform: uppercase;">Hillary, SEPTEMBER 25, 2015
